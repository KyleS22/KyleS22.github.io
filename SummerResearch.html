Summer Research Project: P2IRC

I had the opportunity to spend my summer working in the DISCUS lab on the P2IRC project at the University of Saskatchewan. My project for the summer was to create a prototype sensor data hub to collect data from sensors out in a field. We repurposed some old bluetooth sensors to interface with our system. I used a raspberry pi with bluetooth capabilities to collect the data. I set up a database, and a system for connecting to the pi from anywhere on campus. The system was to go out in a field for collecting data on canola. A large issue with canola fields is their general lack of power. To solve this, I connected the pi to a large battery that charged itself from a solar panel. I then made the system waterproof and we deployed for a demo.

We also added the ability to connect a USB camera to the system. This allowed us to live stream the field to our lab for image analysis. Overall the prototype worked, and it was a fun project to work on.

For more information on the P2IRC project, you can visit their site: http://p2irc.usask.ca/
